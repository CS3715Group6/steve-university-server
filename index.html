Our Campuses

Northern Campus

The Steve University Northern Campus (SUNC) is a four-year undergraduate, graduate, and post-graduate campus of the Steve University. It is located in downtown Steve, Newfoundland & Labrador. Students can choose majors in business, education, nursing, computer science, information technology, criminal justice, social work, environmental science, urban studies and interdisciplinary arts and sciences.
Visit the Northern Campus.

Update Description

Southern Campus

The Steve University Southern Campus (SUSC) is a four-year undergraduate and graduate campus in Steve, one of the three campuses of the Steve University. SUSC is the largest branch campus in Canada and the fastest growing four-year university in Newfoundland & Labrador. In 2014 and 2015, Steve Magazine ranked SUSC as the best university in Newfoundland & Labrador in terms of value and quality.
Visit the Southern Campus.

Update Description

Eastern Campus

The Steve University Eastern Campus (SUEC), located in the suburb of Steve, began in the 1970s. Many academic programs, including the entirety of the undergraduate College of Arts and Sciences, the Steve University Law School, the School of Engineering and Applied Sciences, the School of Management, the Graduate School of Social Work, and the Graduate School of Education, as well as Lockwood Memorial Library, Capen Library, and many administrative offices, are located on SUEC.
Visit the Eastern Campus.
